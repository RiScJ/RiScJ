Lecture 6

Waves & Interference Phenomena

How can we definitively demonstrate wavelike behavior? The most obvious answer is to conduct some sort of interference experiment, because interference is (classically) a behavior solely of waves. The prototypical interference experiment is perhaps the double slit experiment:

So long as the distance \(L\) to the screen is much larger than the distance \(d\) between the slits, we can treat the paths between the two sources (the slits) and some point on the screen as if they were perpendicular. This approximation allows us to calculate the extra distance traveled along one of the paths. We can therefore determine how much of a phaseshift will occur between the paths, and therefore to what degree the interference will be destructive or constructive at that point on the screen.
